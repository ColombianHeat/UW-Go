CMH Kitchen Low Exhaust Issues¶

Introduction¶

The kitchen in CMH has been having issues with insufficient exhaust, causing the smoke produced by the grills to remain in the space. The kitchen's exhaust system is made up of two AHUs, AHU-3 and AHU-4. Each AHU has an exhaust fan which is turned on when the cooking day begins. Four ducts feed the fan. However, the fourth duct seems to only open in case of fire, so we will ignore it. Each duct has a damper which modulates to maintain sufficient exhaust for each grille. Each AHU also has a supply fan which should modulate according to the speed of the exhaust fan.

Dampers¶

Each AHU has three exhaust dampers, each serving one grill or portion of one grill. Let's generate a plot tracking their position wrt time.

Damper 2 of AHU-4 has a trend on it, but it seems to have stopped sending data recently. Its trends do not show up in this study.

In both AHUs, Damper 1 looks like it's almost on a timed loop to open and close with a period of exactly one day. Damper 3 is much more erratic in its behaviour. At 10:30 pm each day, all dampers briefly open to 100%, without fail. It looks like this is hard-coded or manually prompted behaviour; see how AHU-4 Damper 3 always goes from its usual value of ~50% straight to 100%, followed by a perfectly steady closing throughout the next morning.

Exhaust and Supply Fans¶

Each exhaust fan modulates according to "the amount of cooking" happening int the kitchen. The sequence of operations isn't super clear on the specifics. See the below plot showing the VFD % status for each fan wrt time.

AHU-3 supply fan trend is a constant flat line at 0%. There is likely some connection issue, as the fan is running, as confirmed by a site visit.

Typical Day¶

To see trends with finer detail, we'll zoom into the two above plots for a random typical day: Friday November 10th. Then we'll get a better idea of at what time events typically occur. Each vertical grid line lines up exactly on the hour.

Conclusions¶

The dampers seem to be modulating well. The supply and exhaust fans seem to also be working as intended, turning on to 100% speed each morning, until the late evening. The cause of our smoky kitchen is not likely to lie in the sequence of operations or anything that could be found via trends.
